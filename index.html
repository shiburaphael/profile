Project 1

Mazak Fluid Solutions

Cleaning Machine

Project:Mazak
Months:7 Months
Date:February 2024
Role:UX Lead

Overview:

Mazak Fluid Solutions specializes in the design and manufacture of products like the Sump SharkÂ® sump cleaners, dumping hoppers, and coolant dispensers that are essential in industrial and manufacturing settings. These solutions are particularly important for maintaining optimal fluid systems in machinery, preventing contamination, and ensuring a safe and efficient production environment. The project will focus on creating a user-friendly app or digital platform for managing and maintaining these products. This could include tracking performance, maintaining fluid levels, ordering replacement parts, and providing timely maintenance notifications.

Project Vision

Target Audience:

  • Manufacturing Operators and Technicians who handle day-to-day operation and maintenance of the machinery.
  • Facility Managers and Maintenance Supervisors responsible for overseeing the fluid systems, managing equipment, and ensuring uptime.
  • Suppliers or Service Technicians who provide parts or servicing for the equipment.

Key Features:

  • Real-time Monitoring: Track the performance of sump cleaners, coolant dispensers, and hoppers in real-time.
  • Maintenance Scheduling & Alerts: Receive notifications about regular maintenance, fluid changes, or performance issues.
  • Order & Inventory Management: Track fluid levels and schedule reorders for fluids or parts needed for the equipment.
  • Usage Reports: Generate reports that analyze equipment efficiency, fluid consumption, and machine downtime.
  • Product Support: Access user manuals, troubleshooting guides, and customer support.

Challenges

  • Integration with Equipment: Ensuring real-time data integration with Mazak machines (Sump SharkÂ®, dumping hoppers, etc.) to track performance and fluid levels.
  • User Needs: The platform needs to cater to different types of users (operators, managers, and suppliers), each with different needs and permissions.
  • Data Accuracy & Timeliness: Ensuring accurate data collection for maintenance schedules, fluid levels, and machine performance to prevent operational downtime.
  • User-Friendly Interface: Operators and technicians may not be tech-savvy, so the interface must be simple and intuitive.
  • Equipment Compatibility: Mazak Fluid Solutions products may vary in model, so the app must support multiple models with customized features.
  • Environmental Concerns: Providing eco-friendly fluid options and tracking fluid waste or recycling capabilities for sustainable operations.
  • Multi-Device Support: The platform must work across mobile devices, tablets, and desktop for flexibility.

Kickoff

  • Stakeholder Meeting: Align on the goals, functionality, and target users. Define what data will be tracked and how it will be presented to users.
  • User Research: Interview operators, maintenance staff, and facility managers to understand pain points, needs, and desires.
  • Market Research: Examine competitors or similar platforms that track and manage industrial equipment and fluids.

Project 2

Gated Community App

Apartment Maintenance

Project:Gated Community App
Months:6 Months
Date:November 2023
Role:UX Lead

Overview:

The goal of the project is to create a comprehensive, user-friendly gated community app that enhances security, improves communication, streamlines management processes, and fosters community engagement. The app, called Gated Community App, will empower residents and management teams by digitizing critical aspects of gated community operations.

Project Vision

Target Audience:

  • Residents:
    1. Tech-Savvy Professionals: Prefer digital solutions for convenience and time-saving.
    2. Elderly Individuals: Need simple, intuitive interfaces to stay informed and connected.
    3. Families: Look for secure environments and tools to manage household activities efficiently.
  • Community Management:
    1. Managers and Administrators: Require tools to streamline operations, monitor community activities, and maintain transparent communication with residents.
    2. Security Staff: Need a system for efficient visitor management and instant alerting in emergencies.
  • Vendors and Service Providers:
    1. Local businesses offering services like cleaning, repairs, or groceries who want easier access to the gated community.

Key Features:

  • Security and Visitor Management
    1. Pre-approved digital gate passes for guests.
    2. Real-time visitor logs with QR code check-ins.
    3. Emergency alert button for residents to notify security.
  • Communication Hub
    1. Centralized announcements for updates and notices.
    2. Direct messaging with management and neighbors.
    3. Multi-language support for diverse communities.
  • Amenities and Facility Management
    1. Online booking for shared resources like clubhouses, pools, or parking spaces.
    2. Maintenance requests submission and tracking.
    3. Notifications for repairs or resource availability.
  • Payment Solutions
    1. Integrated payment gateway for maintenance fees, utilities, and event registrations.
    2. Auto-reminders for pending payments.
    3. Historical transaction records for transparency.
  • Community Engagement
    1. Event management: Registration for community gatherings, workshops, or festivals.
    2. Forums and classifieds for discussions, buying/selling items, or finding services.
    3. Neighborhood directory for connecting with other residents.
  • Emergency Support
    1. One-tap emergency alerts notifying security and key contacts.
    2. Access to emergency numbers (ambulance, fire, police).
  • Analytics and Reports
    1. For management: Visitor trends, payment summaries, and resource usage analytics.
    2. For residents: Personal history of bookings, payments, and participation in community activities.

Challenges

The project encountered several challenges during its planning and development:

  • Diverse User Needs:
    1. Residents ranged from tech-savvy millennials to elderly individuals unfamiliar with apps.
    2. Multilingual requirements due to cultural diversity in the community.
  • Security Concerns:
    1. Ensuring data privacy and security for resident information.
    2. Robust visitor authentication to prevent unauthorized access.
  • Integration Complexity:
    1. Existing legacy systems for community management had limited APIs.
    2. Real-time synchronization between management software, payment gateways, and the app.
  • Adoption Resistance:
    1. Some residents preferred traditional processes over digital systems.
    2. Skepticism about app reliability and data security.
  • Budget Constraints:
    1. Balancing advanced features with affordability for the community.

Kickoff

  • Participants:
    1. Residents' representatives.
    2. Management staff.
    3. Security team leaders.
    4. Development team and project managers.
  • Activities:
    1. Discussed pain points, project scope, and desired outcomes.
    2. Collected insights into user preferences through surveys and open forums.
    3. Prioritized features using a MoSCoW (Must Have, Should Have, Could Have, Wonât Have) analysis.
  • Outcome:
    1. Finalized a roadmap for development with phased feature rollouts.
    2. Established milestones and timelines.

Project 3

Rental House

Find your perfect place

Project:Rental House
Months:6 Months
Date:July 2024
Role:UX Lead

Overview:

The goal of the Rental House app is to streamline the process of finding, exploring, and securing rental properties. The app will provide users with a tailored search experience based on preferences such as budget, location, property type, and amenities. It will also include features such as virtual tours, neighborhood insights, and booking viewing appointments.

Project Vision

Target Audience:

  • Renters and TechniciansPeople looking for apartments, houses, or condos.
  • LandlordsProperty owners who want to list their properties for rent.
  • Real Estate Agents Professionals who manage multiple listings and help renters find suitable properties.

Key Features:

  • Search Filters: Budget, location, number of bedrooms, pet-friendliness, amenities (e.g., parking, gym).
  • Property Listings: Listings with images, detailed descriptions, and virtual tours.
  • Map View:Interactive map to see the property's location in relation to key areas like schools, work, public transport, etc.
  • Booking Appointments: Ability to book viewings directly from the listing.
  • Save and Share Properties: Users can save their favorite listings and share them with others.
  • Rental Application Process: Option to apply for the property directly from the app.

Challenges

  • Complex Search Filters: Finding the balance between providing detailed filters (e.g., square footage, amenities) without overwhelming the user.
  • Data Accuracy: Ensuring that the property data (availability, prices, photos) is up-to-date and accurate.
  • User Trust: Users need reliable information on properties, landlords, and neighborhoods to make informed decisions.
  • Real-Time Updates: Ensuring that properties listed as available are actually available when users try to book viewings.
  • User Accessibility: The app must be easy to navigate for a broad range of users, including those with disabilities (e.g., colorblind users, text-to-speech functionality).
  • Location Accuracy: For users relying on location-based searches, delivering accurate property locations on maps and proximity to nearby amenities.

Kickoff

  • Stakeholder Meeting:Align on project goals, features, and timeline.
  • Market Research:Analyze current trends in rental platforms (e.g., Zillow, Apartments.com) and identify areas for improvement.
  • User Research:Conduct surveys, interviews, or user testing to understand the pain points and needs of potential users.
  • Defining Success Metrics:Key metrics might include user retention rate, number of bookings made, user satisfaction with the search experience, etc.
